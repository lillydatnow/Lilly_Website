Hobbies

  • Mock Trial. I've been on the San Rafael Mock Trial Team for four years. The last three years I was the pretrial attorney, which means that I argued one on one with a real judge about the admissibility of evidence into a trial. I won the country championships the last three years and have won multiple awards for excellence.
  • Youth Court. Youth Court is a restorative alternative to the traditional juevenile justice system. Youth who have had run-ins with the law and have admitted responsibility can opt to be a part of this program and receive community service instead of more pejorative punisments, and if they complete it in a timely manner, they will have no record. Volunteers in this program can serve as jury members or be trained as advocates and act as a lawyer on behalf of the respondent or the community.
  • Midrasha's Fire Circle. Fire Circle is a group that meets weekly around a bonfire under the open stars to discuss life's big questions.
  • Theater. I've been engaged with theater on and off but have been an enthusiast my entire life. Last year, I was a part of the school's production of The Addam's Family and will also be in the cast of the upcoming musical.
  • Yoga
  • Volunteering and Activism. I've been involved with different volunteer organizations throughout high school, including being a chef for the Ceres Project, an assistant for Halleck Creek Equestrian Therapy for the Disabled, and an algebra tutor. I also have done a number of political internships and will have worked at every level of the United States government by the end of high school. I also frequently attend marches against the inaction over climate change, have planned a walkout of my own protesting the inaction over gun violence, and overall, try to stay up to date on current events and do my part for the world.
